# TiB AI Coach — App Walkthrough Script

Use this script to describe the product to clients, stakeholders, or new team members.  
It is written in plain language — no technical jargon.

---

## Opening (30 seconds)

> **TiB AI Coach** is a private online portal for trade business owners who are part of the Trade in Business community.
>
> Think of it as three things in one place: a **business coach you can chat with anytime**, a **personal action plan** so advice turns into real tasks, and a **hub for events and news** from the TiB team.
>
> Members sign in with email and password. Everything inside the app is personal to them — their chats, their tasks, their progress.

---

## Part 1 — The main app (what members see every day)

### Home screen

> When someone logs in, they land on **Home**.
>
> They see a friendly greeting — for example, *“What do you need help with today, Sarah?”* — and a big green card that says **Start Coaching Session**.
>
> Below that are **six quick-start topics**: Cash Flow, Team, Pricing, Systems, Time, and Growth. One tap opens the coach with that question already typed in.
>
> On the same page they also see **Your Action Plan** — up to four open tasks pulled from their list. They can tick a task off right there, or go to the full Tasks page.
>
> Home is designed to answer one question: *“What should I do right now?”* — either talk to the coach or knock over a task.

### Navigation

> On the left (or in the menu on mobile) members move between five areas:
>
> - **Home** — dashboard and quick start  
> - **AI Coach** — chat with the coach  
> - **Tasks** — full action plan  
> - **Calendar** — upcoming TiB events  
> - **News** — updates from Nicole and the team  
>
> Only admin users see an extra **Admin** link.

### AI Coach

> The **AI Coach** works like a messaging app — similar to ChatGPT, but trained for trade businesses and wired into TiB’s own content.
>
> Members can start a **new chat** or open **past conversations** from a list on the left. Every chat is saved automatically.
>
> They type a question — cash flow, hiring, pricing, systems, whatever they’re stuck on — and the coach replies in real time.
>
> A good answer isn’t just text. The coach can include:
>
> - **Sources** — links to relevant TiB videos, session recordings, or PDF guides  
> - **Next steps** — a short numbered list of what to do next  
> - **Tasks** — action items that are **saved straight into their Action Plan**  
> - **More from TiB** — extra links to videos, podcasts, or tools  
>
> So coaching doesn’t end when the chat ends. The member leaves with sources to read, steps to follow, and tasks already on their list.

### Tasks (Action Plan)

> **Tasks** is the member’s personal to-do list.
>
> Tasks can come from two places: the coach suggests them during a chat, or the member adds them manually with **Add Task**.
>
> Each task has a title and optional notes. Tasks from the coach are marked **AI Generated**.
>
> Members filter by **All**, **Open**, or **Done**, tick items complete, and see a **completion percentage** at the top. There’s also a short motivational **Coach’s Note** when they still have open items.
>
> The idea is simple: coaching advice becomes accountable work, not forgotten chat history.

### Calendar

> **Calendar** shows **upcoming TiB events** — workshops, expert sessions, momentum meets, and similar.
>
> It’s a clean list grouped by month, not a complicated calendar grid. Each event shows the date, time, location, and a short description. Some events are marked **Featured**.
>
> If registration is open, there’s a **Register** button that takes them to the signup page (often on the main TiB website).
>
> Past events drop off automatically — members only see what’s still ahead of them.

### News

> **News** is a feed of **articles and updates** from Nicole and Trade in Business.
>
> Posts can include an image, date, title, and body. The newest posts get a **New** badge. Long articles can be expanded with **Read more**.
>
> It’s the “what’s happening in the community” page — separate from the coach, but part of the same member experience.

## Part 2 — Popups, events, and calendar (how they fit together)

> This trips people up sometimes, so it’s worth saying clearly: **popups, calendar events, and news are three different things.**

### Popups (announcements)

> A **popup** is a message that appears **on top of the app** when a member opens it — on any page, not just Home.
>
> It’s used for timely announcements: a workshop this week, a new program, an important reminder.
>
> The popup shows a tag (like *Event* or *Workshop*), a title, a one-line summary, optional longer description, and sometimes a date.
>
> The member reads it and taps **Got it** (or closes it). **They only see each popup once** — after they dismiss it, it doesn’t keep coming back on that device.
>
> Admins control **when** a popup runs: they set a **show from** date and an optional **hide after** date, and turn it **published** when it should go live.

### Calendar events (on the Calendar page)

> **Calendar events** are **not** popups. They live on the **Calendar** page as a permanent list members can browse anytime.
>
> Good for: “Here’s everything coming up in the next few months.”
>
> Admins add title, date, time, location, description, optional registration link, and can mark an event as **featured**.

### How popups and calendar work together

> You might use **both** for the same workshop:
>
> - **Popup** — “Don’t miss this — register now!” when they first log in this week  
> - **Calendar event** — the full details stay on the Calendar page for anyone who wants to look later  
>
> They’re managed separately in Admin, but they support the same member journey: **see it → remember it → register or show up**.

## Part 3 — Admin dashboard (for the TiB team)

### Who gets in

> **Admin** is only for trusted TiB staff — not regular members.
>
> If you’re an admin, you see an **Admin** link in the menu. Everyone else doesn’t see it and can’t open those pages.

### Admin home

> The admin area has three sections:
>
> 1. **Calendar events** — what appears on the member Calendar page  
> 2. **News posts** — what appears on the member News page  
> 3. **Announcements** — popup messages when members open the app  
>
> Each section uses the same pattern: a **form on the left** to create or edit, and a **list on the right** of everything already there.

### Managing calendar events

> To add an event, an admin fills in:
>
> - Title  
> - Date and time  
> - Location (e.g. Online, or a city)  
> - Description  
> - Registration link (optional)  
> - **Featured** — tick this to highlight it on the member Calendar page  
>
> They can **edit** or **delete** any event. Changes show up for all members on the Calendar page. Admins see past and future events; members only see upcoming ones.

### Managing news posts

> To publish news, an admin adds:
>
> - Title  
> - Body text  
> - Image URL (optional)  
> - **Published** — off means draft (members don’t see it); on means live on the News page  
>
> Good for program updates, success stories, or longer announcements that don’t need a popup.

### Managing popup announcements

> To run a popup, an admin sets:
>
> - Tag (e.g. Event, News, Workshop)  
> - Title and summary  
> - Longer description (optional)  
> - Event date (optional — shown in the popup)  
> - **Show from** and **Hide after** — the live window  
> - **Published** — must be on for members to see it  
>
> The app shows eligible popups when a member loads any page, one at a time, until they dismiss them.

## Part 4 — The full member journey (story you can tell)

> Here’s the story end to end:
>
> 1. A tradie **signs up** and logs into TiB AI Coach.  
> 2. They might see a **popup** about an upcoming workshop.  
> 3. On **Home**, they tap **Cash Flow** or **Start Coaching Session**.  
> 4. In the **coach**, they ask what’s keeping them awake at night. The coach answers with TiB sources, next steps, and **auto-saved tasks**.  
> 5. On **Tasks**, they work through those items and tick them off.  
> 6. On **Calendar**, they find the workshop they heard about and hit **Register**.  
> 7. On **News**, they read the latest update from Nicole.  
> 8. Next week they come back, open an **old chat**, or start a new one — and the cycle continues.
>
> The app’s job is to turn **coaching content** into **daily action** and keep members **connected to live events and community news** — without them hunting across emails, Facebook, and random PDFs.

## Closing (15 seconds)

> **In one line:** TiB AI Coach is where trade business owners get personalised coaching, turn advice into tasks, and stay on top of TiB events and news — all in one signed-in portal.
>
> **For admins:** the same portal has a simple back office to publish events, news, and popups — no developer needed for day-to-day updates.

## Quick reference — member vs admin

| What | Member sees | Admin manages |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| Coaching chat | AI Coach page | — (content comes from knowledge base) |
| Tasks | Home + Tasks page | — (personal per user) |
| Upcoming events | Calendar page | Admin → Calendar events |
| News articles | News page | Admin → News posts |
| One-time alerts | Popup on app open | Admin → Announcements |

## Demo flow (if you’re showing the app live)

1. Log in as a **member** → show Home greeting and quick prompts.  
2. Open **AI Coach** → ask one question → point out sources, next steps, and tasks.  
3. Open **Tasks** → show the task that appeared from the chat.  
4. Open **Calendar** → scroll an upcoming event → click Register if linked.  
5. Open **News** → show a published post.  
6. Log in as **admin** → show the three admin sections and create/edit one item (draft, don’t publish if live demo).  
7. Optional: show a **popup** by publishing a short announcement with today’s date in the show window.
